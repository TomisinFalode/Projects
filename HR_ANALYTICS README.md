# HR Analytics: Employee Attrition Analysis

This project explores the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset to uncover patterns and insights around
why employees leave an organization.
The aim is to support HR and business leaders in making data-driven decisions that improve employee retention.

## Dataset

- Source: [Kaggle - IBM HR Analytics Attrition Dataset]
(https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset)
- Filename: `WA_Fn-UseC_-HR-Employee-Attrition.csv`
- Records: 1,470 employees
- Key Features: Age, Attrition, Department, MonthlyIncome, JobRole, OverTime, YearsAtCompany, etc.

## Project Goals

- Identify patterns and correlations related to employee attrition.
- Visualize trends by department, gender, income, and job role.
- Generate actionable recommendations for improving employee retention.

## Key Insights

- **OverTime**: Strongly associated with higher attrition.
- **Monthly Income**: Lower-income employees are more likely to leave.
- **Job Role**: Roles like Sales Rep and Lab Tech show higher attrition.
- **Years at Company**: New hires are at higher risk of leaving early.

## Business Recommendations

- Review and potentially adjust overtime expectations.
- Improve compensation packages for high-turnover roles.
- Strengthen onboarding and early-stage engagement programs.
- Monitor new employees more closely to reduce first-year attrition.
